Lesson Plan

Resources required:

  • one Marty robot for every group of 3-5 children (V1 or V2)
  • Marty's skate attachment (3D printable, download file here)
  • A computer for each group, set up on Scratch 3.0 online

Preparation ahead of class

Be aware you will need to make some small preparations in advance of the lesson!

The skates

You will need to either be provided with or 3D print your own skate feet for Marty (files for printing linked above). Unzip the file once downloaded and print two copies of each file. These should be compatible with both V1 and V2s Martys. The base skate can simply be attached in place of his feet. The axles and wheels can be attached to the skates. (Add pictures here for construction)

Connecting to Marty

It's also suggested you take a little time to familiarise yourself with the process of connecting to Marty in advance of the session - he can be a little temperamental on occasion. This session will use Marty's block-based coding interface in Scratch, and expects no prior knowledge. View Robotical's page here on connecting to Marty and give it a shot in advance of the session. Also make sure all robots are fully charged!

Introduction

Show students this video of Marty getting used to his skates!
Explain to students they will be helping your very own Marty robots to learn to skate!

Coding (20 mins)

Show the students how to connect to and operate Marty.
Allow them to experiment with controlling his movements. Encourage children to pass around the controller and share. Use basic blocks to encode a sequence of moves.

Exploration (15 minutes)

Skating!

Let the students attempt to skate with their Marty robots.
