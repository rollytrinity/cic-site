Lesson Plan

Resources required:

  • a Marty robot
  • Marty's skate attachment (3d printable, see file here)
  • materials to make skates

Introduction

Show students this video of Marty getting used to his skates!
Explain to students they will be helping your very own Marty robots to learn to skate - but first they need skates!

Coding (20 mins)

Show the students how to connect to and operate Marty.
Allow them to experiment with controlling his movements. Encourage children to pass around the controller and share. Use basic blocks to encode a sequence of moves.

Exploration (15 minutes)

Put away any devices used for Marty's control to remove distraction. Split students into groups of 3-5 and ask them to take a look at the provided materials. Which pieces do they think could help make skates for Marty? Encourage them to develop a plan for their design.
Questions for students:
  • Which wheels should they use?
  • How will they create an axle? Do they know what an axle is?
  • How will they attach the different components?

Building (40 minutes)

Instruct students to enact their plans (or continue developing them).
Encourage students to work on their ideas collaboratively, without resolving issues for them. Towards the end of the session, if you feel adult intervention is needed, offer assistance.

Skating!

Let the students attempt to skate with their Marty robots.
