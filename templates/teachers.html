Lesson Plan

Resources required:

  • One Marty robot for every group of 2-4 children (V1 or V2)
  • Marty's skate attachment (3D printable, download files here)
  • A computer for each group, set up for Scratch programming with Marty (Scratch programming environment for Marty V1 here, alternative for Marty V2 here)
Not sure if you have a Marty V1 or V2? If you or your school acquired the Marty robots from 2020 onwards, they are likely to be V2. Note that this lesson plan was designed using a V1 Marty, but it should be compatible with V2 Marty including 3D printed parts - just be sure to double check connection and programming environments.

Preparation ahead of class

Be aware you will need to make some small preparations in advance of the lesson!

The skates

You will need to either be provided with or 3D print your own skate feet for Marty (files for printing linked above).
Steps
  1. Extract the downloaded files. Print two copies of each file.
  2. Slide the axles through the holes under the skates, and attach the wheels (add pics).
  3. Unscrew Marty's feet, and replace them with the skates.

Connecting to Marty

It's also suggested you take a little time to familiarise yourself with the process of connecting to Marty in advance of the session - he can be a little temperamental on occasion. This session will use Marty's block-based coding interface in Scratch, and expects no prior knowledge. View Robotical's page here on connecting to Marty and give it a shot in advance of the session. Also make sure all robots are fully charged!

Introduction

Show students this video of Marty getting used to his skates!
Explain to students they will be helping your very own Marty robots to learn to skate!

Setup (5-10 mins)

Show the students how to connect to Marty in Scratch, ideally displaying this at the front of the class on your own computer. Show them where they can find Marty's coding blocks (below all other Scratch blocks). Explain that we can use the computer to give instructions to Marty that let him move. Begin with the simple code block below to show them how to move Marty's arms (save the walking for them to discover!) and click on the green flag to begin:

Exploration (10 minutes)

Allow the students to experiment with Marty's code blocks. They will likely be excited to try a number of motions. Students here will naturally grasp the concept of sequence in programming, the idea that each instruction is executed one after another.

Introduction to Loops (10 minutes)

By now, the robots will likely be moving about in chaotic fashion! Stop the class and ask them to switch off their robots while you speak. Now present them with the Control block of Scratch. Add a block with a repeat loop first, iterating three times. Then present them with a forever loop.

Skating! (10 minutes)

Let the students attempt to skate with their Marty robots. Now encourage them to incorporate loops into their code.

Cone challenge

Create a set of points to weave around for each group. Ask them to try and weave through the cones as fast as they are able.

The race!

Challenge the groups to race through the cones! See which Marty skates best!

Reflection

Provide the students with the reflection sheet which will ask them a few short questions about what they have done.
